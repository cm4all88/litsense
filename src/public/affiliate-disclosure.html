Affiliate Disclosure

Effective April 25, 2026  ·  Last updated April 25, 2026

FTC DISCLOSURE — 16 CFR PART 255

LitSense participates in affiliate advertising programs. When you click certain links on LitSense and make a purchase, we may earn a commission at no additional cost to you. We only link to books and products we would genuinely recommend — affiliate relationships never influence our editorial recommendations or Sage's suggestions.

Amazon Associates Program

LitSense is a participant in the Amazon Services LLC Associates Program, an affiliate advertising program designed to provide a means for sites to earn advertising fees by advertising and linking to Amazon.com.

When you click a "Buy on Amazon" link or any other link to Amazon.com from LitSense:

  • You will be taken to Amazon's website to complete any purchase.
  • Amazon will track the referral and credit LitSense with a commission (typically 1%–10% of the purchase price, depending on product category) if you make a qualifying purchase.
  • The price you pay on Amazon is not affected by our affiliate relationship.
  • Amazon's own Privacy Notice and Conditions of Use govern your interactions on Amazon's platform.

Other Affiliate Programs

From time to time, LitSense may also participate in affiliate programs with other booksellers and literary services, including but not limited to:

  • Bookshop.org (supports independent bookstores)
  • ThriftBooks
  • Audible (audiobooks)

In all cases, the same principle applies: you pay the same price you would otherwise, and LitSense may earn a commission from qualifying purchases. Any such links will be used only for books and services we believe are genuinely valuable to readers.

Our Editorial Independence

The books and authors recommended by LitSense — including all recommendations generated by Sage — are selected solely based on their merit and your personal reading preferences. No publisher, author, or retailer pays LitSense to recommend specific books. Affiliate relationships are passive: we earn commissions only when users organically click through and purchase books we've already recommended.

We do not accept payment for placement in recommendation lists, sponsored reviews, or feature positions in our curated rows. If that ever changes, we will clearly disclose sponsored content at the point of display.

What This Means for You

We want to be completely transparent about how LitSense makes money. Our revenue comes from:

  • LitSense Pro subscriptions — our primary revenue source
  • Marketplace transaction fees — a 10% fee on book swap sales
  • Affiliate commissions — small commissions when you purchase books through our links

Affiliate commissions are a supplementary revenue source and do not drive our recommendation logic. Sage recommends books it calculates are the best match for you — not the most profitable referral.

Identifying Affiliate Links

Any link that takes you to an external retailer to purchase a book may be an affiliate link. These are currently labeled with a "Buy" button or retailer name (e.g., "Buy on Amazon"). We do not obscure or hide the fact that links lead to retailer sites.
