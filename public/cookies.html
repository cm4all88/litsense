Cookie & Storage Notice

Effective April 25, 2026  ·  Last updated April 25, 2026

LitSense does not use advertising cookies or sell your browsing data. We use browser storage only to make the app work and remember your preferences.

What We Use & Why

LitSense uses cookies (small files stored in your browser) and localStorage (browser-side storage) to operate the Service and remember your preferences. We do not use third-party advertising trackers or sell cookie data.

Cookies

Cookies are set by the following parties when you use LitSense:

Cookie Name / Pattern Set By Purpose Duration
__session, __clerk_* Clerk Authentication — keeps you signed in Required Session / 1 year
__stripe_* Stripe Fraud prevention and payment security Required Session / 1 year
Vercel analytics Vercel Aggregated performance analytics — no personal identification Optional 90 days

LitSense does not set any advertising or retargeting cookies. We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or similar advertising trackers.

localStorage & sessionStorage

LitSense stores the following data in your browser's local storage. This data stays on your device and is not transmitted to our servers unless explicitly used in a feature:

Key Purpose Type
ls_welcome_shown Tracks whether you've seen the welcome screen so it only shows once Functional
ls_counter Daily usage counter for free tier rate limiting Functional
ls_onboarding_* Saves your progress through the onboarding flow Functional
ls_prefs_* UI preferences (theme, layout settings) Functional
Clerk session tokens Authentication state — required to stay signed in Essential

Third-Party Cookies

When you click affiliate links to external retailers like Amazon, those sites may set their own cookies. LitSense has no control over third-party cookies set on external websites. Please refer to those sites' own cookie policies for details.

Your Choices

Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. Note that blocking essential cookies (authentication) will prevent you from signing in to LitSense.

  • Chrome cookie settings
  • Firefox cookie settings
  • Safari cookie settings
  • Edge cookie settings

localStorage

You can clear localStorage data through your browser's developer tools or by clearing your browser's site data for litsense.vercel.app. This will reset your welcome screen status, usage counters, and UI preferences. You will not lose account data or your saved shelf — that is stored in Supabase and tied to your account.

Do Not Track

LitSense respects Do Not Track (DNT) signals where technically feasible. We do not engage in cross-site tracking regardless of DNT settings, as we do not use cross-site advertising trackers.
